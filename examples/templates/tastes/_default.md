# _default.md — universal preferences

*Curated over time. Last revised: 2027-04-15. ~25 entries.*

This file always loads at session start, regardless of which image you're working on. Holds preferences that apply across genres — workflow notes, communication style, cross-genre aversions, settings that should be the agent's defaults.

For genre-specific preferences (underwater, wildlife, portrait, landscape, …), see the sibling files in this directory. The brief.md declares which genre files apply to each image.

---

## Color (cross-genre)

- Don't lift the green channel under any circumstances; it reads sickly. (2026-09-03)
- Warm tones on subjects with natural warmth (rocks, coral, terracotta, fur) are fine, even slight orange — not a problem the way warm casts on neutral subjects would be. (2026-11-14)

## Tone (cross-genre)

- Crush deep shadows to true black. Doesn't bother me; I want the contrast. (2026-05-02)
- Lift shadows on subjects, almost always. Subjects need detail in their darker zones. (2026-05-02)

## Sharpening / structure (cross-genre)

- `structure_subtle` for textured surfaces (skin, scales, fur, rough rock). Never `structure_strong` — it reads aggressive. (2026-06-10)
- `sharpen_standard` is fine on output; pre-sharpening with structure has to be subtle to avoid haloing. (2026-06-10)

## Aesthetic / mood

- Quiet over dramatic for animal portraits. Let the subject be the story. (2026-05-02)
- Grain is fine and even welcome at high ISO; don't over-denoise. (2026-09-03)
- Crop tight when the subject earns it; loose when the environment is part of the story. (2027-01-09)

## What I avoid

- Vivid / saturated looks. Reads tropical, reads commercial, not me. (2026-05-12)
- Heavy clarity / Lightroom-style "punch." (2026-05-12)
- Symmetric vignettes. They flatten the frame. Subtle / asymmetric only. (2026-08-18)

## Workflow notes

- I prefer to see 2–3 candidates before committing to a direction; branching is encouraged. (2026-06-10)
- When I correct color targets, you tend to estimate slightly cool. Bias warmer when in doubt. (2026-09-03)
- I don't use Mode B much; my work is per-image. Don't push autonomous exploration unless I ask. (2027-02-14)
- Each session, surface at most 1–2 patterns you noticed for taste.md additions. Not more. (2027-03-04)
