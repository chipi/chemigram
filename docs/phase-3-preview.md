# Phase 3 Preview — Parametric masks in vocabulary

> A guide to thinking about Phase 3 while you're in Phase 2. Heads up: Phase 3 may dissolve into Phase 2 entirely — you might just author parametric-masked entries from day one of Phase 2 and never have a separate "Phase 3" transition. This page is for understanding the move, not waiting for permission.

## What Phase 3 is

Phase 1 shipped raster mask support: the agent can call `generate_mask(image_id, target="manta")` and the bundled `CoarseAgentProvider` produces a coarse PNG (or `chemigram-masker-sam` produces a precise one in Phase 4). That's one kind of mask.

darktable supports two more kinds, and they're deeper than they look.

**Phase 3 is the realization that vocabulary entries can carry their own masks** — not raster ones generated by an AI per-image, but *parametric* or *drawn* ones captured at authoring time and applied universally. The vocabulary entry says "lift shadows in luminance zones 0–30%" or "warm the water based on hue range" rather than "lift shadows in this masked region."

The engine work was already done in Phase 1. The contribution is purely vocabulary.

## Three kinds of masks in darktable

| Mask kind | What it is | Authored | Cost |
|-|-|-|-|
| **Drawn** | Hand-drawn shapes (gradient, ellipse, path, brush) baked into the dtstyle | At capture time, by the photographer in darktable | Cheap; portable across images; not subject-aware |
| **Parametric** | Range-based selection (luminance, chroma, hue, lightness) baked into blendop_params | At capture time, in darktable's blend GUI | Cheap; portable; semi-content-aware (responds to image stats) |
| **Raster** | A PNG generated per-image by an AI provider | At session time, by the agent | Expensive; per-image; pixel-precise |

Phase 1 Slice 4 shipped raster mask support. Drawn and parametric masks were always available — they're just blendop_params blobs that the synthesizer treats as opaque. **Phase 3 is the work of authoring vocabulary entries that carry drawn or parametric masks.**

## What parametric-masked vocabulary looks like

Examples that fit the "module_intention_context" naming convention:

| Name | Mask shape | Use case |
|-|-|-|
| `expo_+0.5_shadows_only` | Parametric, lightness range 0–35% | Lift exposure restricted to dark zones |
| `tone_lift_highlights_clipped` | Parametric, lightness range 80–100% | Recover highlights without touching mid-tones |
| `wb_warm_water_only` | Parametric, hue range cyan-blue | Warm only the water in underwater shots |
| `colorcal_neutral_skin_only` | Parametric, hue range orange-pink, saturation > 20% | Neutralize skin while leaving the background colors alone |
| `gradient_top_dampen_highlights` | Drawn, top-of-frame gradient | Reduce harsh top light (sun, sky) |
| `gradient_bottom_warm_underwater` | Drawn, bottom-of-frame gradient | Warm the foreground in over-cool shots |
| `vignette_subtle` | Drawn, ellipse centered | Subtle attention-pulling vignette |
| `parametric_warm_only_highlights` | Parametric, lightness 70–100% | Warm only the bright zones (golden-hour feel) |

**These are entries you can author today.** The engine doesn't need anything new; you just open darktable, set up the parametric mask in the blend tab, capture the style, and add a manifest entry.

## How parametric and raster masks differ

Same module (e.g., shadow lift), different mask kinds:

```
tone_lifted_shadows                   ← unmasked: lifts ALL shadows globally
tone_lifted_shadows_lightness         ← parametric: lifts shadows in lightness 0–35%
                                        (works on every image, no AI)
tone_lifted_shadows_subject           ← raster: lifts shadows on AI-detected subject
                                        (per-image, requires masker, pixel-precise)
```

The first two are vocabulary entries. The third is a vocabulary entry + an agent-generated mask at runtime. **All three coexist** — you'd reach for different ones in different situations.

Rule of thumb:

- Want to lift shadows everywhere → unmasked entry
- Want to lift dark zones only → parametric entry (cheap, universal, no AI)
- Want to lift shadows on the manta only → raster entry + AI mask (expensive, image-specific)

The Phase 3 insight is: **a lot of moves you currently do as raster masks would work fine as parametric masks**, with no AI cost. "Warm the highlights" doesn't need to know what the highlights *are*; it just needs the lightness range.

## What to watch for in Phase 2 that signals Phase 3

As you run sessions in Phase 2, watch for these moments:

### Strong signal — Phase 3 is right here

- **The agent reaches for a raster mask but a parametric one would do the job.** "Generate mask for the bright zones" → really you mean "luminance 70–100%". Parametric is cheaper and works on every image.
- **You log gaps that have a clear range condition.** "Need a move that warms only the cyan parts" / "lift shadows but not the deepest blacks" / "saturation boost but not on skin." These are parametric masks waiting to be authored.
- **Same masked move keeps coming up across different images.** "On every underwater shot I want to warm the water" — that's a parametric WB move (`wb_warm_water_only`), not 50 raster masks.

### Medium signal

- **You notice the agent describing a region in terms of *properties* of the pixels** (their lightness, their hue) rather than *what they are*. "The brightest 20% are too hot" / "the green tones look off" — both parametric.

### Weak signal

- **Single-image gaps with a regional component.** Could be parametric, could be drawn, could be raster. Watch if it recurs.

### Not a Phase 3 signal — actually Phase 4

- **Subject-aware masking.** "Mask the manta" / "the bird's eye" / "this person's face" — these need raster masks. Parametric can't isolate "this animal vs the other animal at the same luminance." Phase 4 handles these via SAM.

## How to dabble during Phase 2

You don't need to wait for Phase 3 to start. The engine already supports parametric masks (they're just `blendop_params`). The procedure:

1. **In darktable's GUI**, open a representative photo for the move you want to author.
2. **Apply the module** (exposure, color calibration, tone equalizer, etc.).
3. **Click the blend tab** for that module → choose "uniformly" → "parametric mask".
4. **Set up the parametric mask** — pick the range channel (lightness, hue, etc.) and adjust the curves to match what you want masked.
5. **Render preview** in darktable to confirm the move feels right.
6. **Export the style** (Styles → Create New Style → select the module → export).
7. **Drop into your personal pack** at `~/.chemigram/vocabulary/personal/layers/L3/<module>/`.
8. **Add a manifest entry.** No `mask_kind: raster` needed (that's only for AI raster masks); parametric masks are inside `blendop_params` and the engine treats them as opaque (per ADR-008).
9. **Validate** with `./scripts/verify-vocab.sh ~/.chemigram/vocabulary/personal`.
10. **Tell the agent** in your next session: "I added `wb_warm_water_only` — try it on this underwater shot." It'll show up in `list_vocabulary()`.

This is what "Phase 3 may dissolve into Phase 2" means. There's no transition ceremony. You just start authoring entries with parametric masks when you reach for them.

## Compositional power

The real win of parametric vocabulary isn't single moves — it's **composition**. The agent can apply two or three parametric-masked entries in sequence:

```
agent: I'll lift shadows in the dark zones (tone_lift_shadows_lightness),
       then warm the water (wb_warm_water_only), then dampen the
       highlights at the top (gradient_top_dampen_highlights). Render?
```

Each move is targeted (parametric mask), composable (they don't fight each other because they affect different ranges/regions), and reproducible (same dtstyle works on every image). That's vocabulary working at full power.

## What Phase 3 is NOT

- **Not raster mask generation.** That's Phase 1 (CoarseAgentProvider) and Phase 4 (SAM). Phase 3 is mask types that don't need a per-image AI provider.
- **Not engine work.** The engine treats parametric masks as opaque blobs (ADR-008). Phase 3 is purely vocabulary-author work.
- **Not Phase 5.** Phase 5 is *continuous parametric control* via hex encoders (Path C — programmatic generation of `expo_+0.42`). Phase 3 is *static parametric masks* baked into pre-authored vocabulary.
